FAQ

  • Who's in charge here?

    Hackers on the Hill is run by the all-volunteer I Am The Cavalry initiative - a group of dedicated individuals from the cybersecurity researcher community. Our goal with this event is to provide you with key resources for cybersecurity knowledge, broker connections to the wide-ranging expertise of the cybersecurity research community, and improve understanding among policymakers and the technical community.

  • When will the event start?

    An excellent question! We're still working out the details, and we intend to have a schedule posted here and through email soon.

  • Will there be a happy hour again?

    Happy hour? Us? ;) Yes, we're planning something similar to the happy hour we had in prior years. We'll post details here and send an email update as they become available.

  • What's the event like, do you have a video or write up of last year, or any prep material?

    • Watch the plenary sessions from 2022 and 2023 on our Hackers on the Hill YouTube channel. (don't forget to smash like and subscribe)
    • Review the Guide to Meeting With Congressional Offices (pdf link) prepared by Harley Geiger.
    • Read our Overview of Hackers on the Hill 2023, on the I Am The Cavalry blog.
    • Take a walk down memory lane with our sites from 2020, 2022, and 2023.

  • Will we be doing the White House this year too?

    Unfortunately not this year. For context, in 2023, the White House invited a select number of hackers for an afternoon session to discuss several cybersecurity topics (and published an official White House Readout on collaborating with cybersecurity researchers!).

  • Where can I park near the event?

    That's a great question!. Parking near the Capitol tends to be extremely limited, and available spots can be a bit of a hike. Check out the US Capitol Visitor Center site for more.

  • What is the photo/video policy? Can I live blog the plenary?

    Thanks for checking first! Everybody loves photos. Except when they don't. And some of them won't. So ask first and try to avoid crowd shots. As far as blogging (tweeting/Xing, tooting, skeeting, whatever), we will announce the ground rules at the event - the plenary is likely to be on the record, the briefings are off the record.

  • Where should I stay nearby - any hotels you recommend?

    There are several hotels nearby the Capitol. We don't have any recommendations, as we haven't tried any of them out.
